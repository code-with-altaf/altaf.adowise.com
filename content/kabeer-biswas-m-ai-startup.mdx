The Indian startup ecosystem is buzzing with the return of one of its most prominent founders. Kabeer Biswas, the former co-founder of Dunzo, has successfully raised ₹102 crore (approximately $11 million) for his latest venture, currently operating under the name **M**. Backed by an all-star roster of investors including Peak XV Partners, Blume Ventures, and Kunal Shah’s CRED, the startup marks a significant pivot from quick-commerce to the frontier of consumer AI.

## From Dunzo to 'M': A Vision for Home Automation

Kabeer Biswas’s journey has been closely tied to the evolution of logistics and services in India. After leading Dunzo and later spearheading Flipkart’s quick-commerce initiative, Flipkart Minutes, until late 2025, Biswas is now focusing on the "friction of managing a home."

**M** (incorporated as Curious Digital Pvt Ltd) is co-founded by Biswas and Kartik Mishra. The startup aims to build a "personal AI concierge" or a consumer AI layer that automates decisions, coordination, and everyday household services. Think of it as "Dunzo 2.0" but powered by an intelligent AI core that doesn't just deliver products, but manages the entire lifecycle of home services, travel bookings, and reservations.

## The Funding Breakdown: Strategic Backing

The ₹102 crore Seed round is led by **Peak XV Partners**, contributing ₹46.4 crore ($5 million). **Blume Ventures** joined with ₹37.12 crore ($4 million), while **CRED** (via Dreamplug Technologies) invested ₹18.56 crore ($2 million). This investment is particularly notable as it marks another direct startup bet for CRED, which has previously backed high-growth firms like Liquiloans and Yubi.

The Bengaluru-based firm is valued at ₹300 crore post-money. Interestingly, the founders have set aside a massive **21.11% ESOP pool**, signaling a strong commitment to attracting top-tier engineering talent to build their AI vision. The majority of the equity (38.67%) is held by the PVK Family Trust, presumably controlled by the founders.

## The AI Concierge Trend

Biswas’s new venture taps into a growing global trend: the move from "Search" to "Action." While traditional assistants provide information, the next generation of consumer AI is expected to execute tasks. By focusing on the "personal concierge" model, **M** aims to become the interface through which users interact with the real world—handling the complexity of coordination so the user doesn't have to.

The entry of Peak XV and Blume into this early-stage AI play underscores the shifting focus of venture capital in India. As quick-commerce matures and enters the consolidation phase, the "Alpha" is now being sought in the orchestration layer—where AI agents manage the very services that logistics companies used to just deliver.
