The Indian consumer market in 2026 is no longer just about buying "things"; it is about protecting them and managing the long-term costs of health and technology. Two companies leading this "Service and Protection" shift are **Truemeds** and **OneAssist**. With significant new capital infusions and rapidly growing revenue, they are defining how Indian consumers manage risk and chronic care.

## Truemeds: Making Chronic Care Affordable

**Truemeds** has successfully secured an additional $20 million from Peak XV Partners as part of its ongoing Series C round. This brings their total funding to over $90 million. The company is tackling one of India's biggest healthcare challenges: the high cost of chronic medication.

By using AI to analyze prescriptions and help consumers discover cost-effective alternative brands, Truemeds is effectively a "discovery engine for affordable health." The strategy is working—Truemeds doubled its revenue to ₹315 crore in FY24 while narrowing its losses. In a market dominated by giants like PharmEasy and Tata 1mg, Truemeds has carved out a niche by focusing ruthlessly on the price-sensitive chronic patient.

## OneAssist: The Protection Platform for the Gadget-Heavy Home

As the average Indian household becomes more gadget-dependent, the demand for protection and post-sales service has exploded. **OneAssist**, backed by Peak XV and Lightspeed, recently announced a ₹108 crore ($12.3 million) funding round from Stride Ventures. 

OneAssist operates a subscription-based model that covers everything from wallet and credit card loss to extended warranties and repairs for mobile phones and home appliances. Their financials reflect the health of the "Protection" market:
- **Revenue Growth**: 22% growth in operating revenue to ₹623 crore in FY25.
- **Loss Reduction**: A massive 56% reduction in net loss, signaling a clear path to profitability.

## The Infrastructure of Convenience

What Truemeds and OneAssist share is a focus on **Convenience and Reliability**. 
- Truemeds provides the convenience of home delivery combined with the reliability of AI-verified alternatives.
- OneAssist provides the reliability of professional repairs and the convenience of a single "Protection Umbrella" for all household assets.

As India's middle class expands, these "Insurance-adjacent" and "Pharmacy-plus" models are becoming the new standard for consumer spending. The trend is clear: consumers are willing to pay for predictability. Whether it's the predictability of a medicine bill or the predictability of a repair cost, the companies that win will be those that remove the "Financial Shock" from the consumer experience.
