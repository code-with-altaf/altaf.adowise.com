When Chegg's stock dropped 99% from its 2021 peak, it became the most dramatic example of AI disrupting an entire business model overnight.

## What Happened to Chegg?

Chegg was the dominant homework-help platform for college students. But when ChatGPT launched, students quickly realized they could get instant, free, personalized explanations — something Chegg's static Q&A database could never match.

By 2025, Chegg had laid off 45% of its workforce. By early 2026, they pivoted entirely to "workforce skilling" — essentially abandoning their core product.

## The Broader Impact on Jobs

Chegg isn't an isolated case. AI is actively disrupting:
- **Customer Support**: AI chatbots now handle 80% of tier-1 support queries.
- **Content Writing**: AI-generated drafts are reviewed by humans, cutting writing teams by 60%.
- **Data Entry & Analysis**: Automated pipelines replace entire departments.
- **Translation Services**: Real-time AI translation has reduced demand for human translators.

## Which Jobs Are Safe?

Roles that require deep human judgment, creativity, and physical presence remain resilient:
- Strategic business consulting
- Complex engineering and architecture
- Healthcare and caregiving
- Creative direction and brand strategy

## The New Reality

The lesson from Chegg is clear: any job that involves organizing and retrieving information is at risk. The future belongs to professionals who can leverage AI as a tool rather than compete against it.
