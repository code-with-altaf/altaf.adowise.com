The Indian FinTech narrative in 2026 has shifted from "User Acquisition" to "Revenue Optimization." We are seeing the emergence of highly profitable wealth platforms and the rapid growth of specialized lending vertical. Leading the charge are platforms like Smallcase, OneCard, and Ambak, each solving a specific piece of the Indian financial puzzle.

## Smallcase: Scaling the WealthTech Stack

**Smallcase** has hit a major milestone, crossing the ₹100 crore revenue mark in FY25. The platform, which helps brokers execute transactions in exchange-traded products, reported ₹106 crore in operating revenue—a 50% year-on-year growth. More importantly, the company has successfully improved its unit economics, reducing EBITDA losses to just ₹9 crore.

With a user base of over 10 million investors and transactions worth ₹1.2 lakh crore facilitated on its platform, Smallcase has become an essential infrastructure layer for the Indian stock market. Its success highlights the growing appetite for "managed" investment products among retail investors in India.

## OneCard and the Credit Expansion

Pune-based **OneCard** (FPL Technologies) continues its upward trajectory, securing an additional $28.5 million from Better Tomorrow Ventures, Peak XV, and Z47. Valued at $1.4 billion, OneCard has managed to grow its revenue six-fold in recent years, reaching ₹593 crore in FY23. 

By targeting first-time credit card users and offering co-branded cards with banks like IDFC First and Federal Bank, OneCard is solving the "Credit Access" problem for India's Gen Z and millennial population. Their integrated OneScore app provides the data-driven foundation for this expansion, creating a seamless lifecycle from credit monitoring to card usage.

## Specialized Lending: Home Loans and Education

The lending market is becoming increasingly verticalized. 
- **Ambak** recently kicked off its Series A with ₹69 crore ($7.84 million) co-led by Peak XV and Z47. The startup is streamlining the home loan process using Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) and bank rule engines, resulting in a 76% surge in valuation to ₹450 crore.
- **Eduvanz**, the education loan specialist, secured a ₹25.5 crore bridge round. While the round happened at a 35% lower valuation (reflecting the broader market correction in EdTech), the firm continues to be a dominant player in financing school fees, coaching, and up-skilling.

## The Outlook: Profitability and Verticalization

The common thread across these success stories is **Vertical Focus**. Whether it is Smallcase’s focus on exchange-traded products, Ambak’s focus on home loans, or OneCard’s focus on mobile-first credit, the "Generalist FinTech" era is over. The winners in 2026 are those who build deep tech stacks for specific financial outcomes. As these companies move toward profitability, we expect to see further consolidation in the market, with profitable "Vertical Leaders" acquiring smaller players to expand their service offerings.
